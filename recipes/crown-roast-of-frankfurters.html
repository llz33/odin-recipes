Crown Roast of Frankfurters Recipe

Description

This one if for those of us that are trying to watch our figure AND watch our wallet.

That said, this recipe is no laughing matter as it was used as the main dish at Prince Charles and Lady Diana's Wedding. Extra pimentos were added to the raw shredded cabbage for the occasion (We wont tell anyone if you decide to add extra too).

So, if you want to live like a king (or queen), but not spend money like one, and have the figure of one, try the Crown Roast of Frankfurters today!

Ingredients

  • 1 pound frankfurters (see Note)
  • 2 tablespoons cider vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon poppy seeds
  • 2 cups shredded cabbage
  • 1/2 cup boiling water
  • Pimento

Directions

Step 1

Slice frankfurters lengthwise, but do not cut all the way through. (Do not separate halves.)

Step 2

Open. Broil, cut sides up, on rack about 3 inches from source of heat, about 5 minutes.

Step 3

Add vinegar and poppy seeds to cabbage; toss thoroughly. Heap cabbage in a mound in center of a baking dish.

Step 4

Lean cut side of frankfurters against cabbage to form a crown. Secure franfurters with toothpicks.

Step 5

Pour water over cabbage. Bake at 350Â° (moderate oven) 10 minutes, or until cabbage is tender but still crisp.

Step 6

Garnish with pimento. Divide evenly. Makes 4 dinner servings.

Note

Note: Frankfurters are pre-cooked. Do not allow 2 ounces for shrinkage.
